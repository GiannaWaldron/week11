The Jungle

Illustrator

These vinyls were originally made for my Color Theory class. The original project brief was to make eight different CD covers with the same design but the colors had to be different. As inspiration we had to look up different patterns and then from there we had to come up with our own design. I mixed together a tropical theme and a bug theme. The top left is monochromatic, the bottom left is split-complementary, the bottom right is analogous, and the top right is any colors I wanted. When editing this project for my portfolio I realized something was missing, so I found a mockup and put my 2D designs on the vinyls to show what they would look like on an actual product.

Broken

Multi-medium Painting

I had found this broken canvas in my room and I was going to throw it out. Then I decided I should make it into something and embrace the rips. I used wax to close some of the rips, as well as sewing them. I was brainstorming what I wanted to put on the canvas and I came up with the topic of fixing things. I remembered the quote “you can’t fix what’s been broken,” and I thought that was perfect for this piece because even though I tried to put the canvas back together it will never go back perfectly the way it was.

Bullied

Paint and Charcoal

Everyone in their life has had some hurtful thing said about them at one time or another. This piece is inspired by that, and the longing for the bullies to stop. This piece is made on cardboard, so I carved out the scratches to show the inside of the cardboard like it is actually being scratched. The scratches symbolize the victim trying to get away, but can’t quite seem to get the help they need.

Seeing Is Knowing

Collage and Painting

In my drawing class we were learning about collages, my professor asked us to create one using the recycled magazines she had. I kept finding black and white images and decided to make it all black and white. I came across “Seeing Is Knowing,” in one of the magazines and thought it was perfect for this, I added the red painting to compliment the red text. The image to the right is a study I did of the collage. I focused on a small part of the collage, which was the bottom right portion, and put black paint where the darkest areas where Then I took another piece of paper and pressed in down on the other to make a stamp of the first painting. I then waited for it to dry and did a continuous contour style drawing of the section.
